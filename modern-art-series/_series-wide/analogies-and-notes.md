# Modern Analogies & Running Notes

Use these to make the art feel immediate and relatable. Add to this as you go.

## Analogies Bank

### Episode 1 — Neo-Classicism
- David was basically a political influencer before social media existed — he shaped public opinion through images
- The Salon was the Instagram of the 18th century — if you weren't on it, you didn't exist
- Winckelmann stanning ancient Greece is like someone today who won't shut up about how everything was better in the '90s

### Episode 2 — Romanticism
- Delacroix vs. Ingres was the Biggie vs. Tupac of 19th-century Paris — two fundamentally different philosophies, one city
- Géricault building a raft in his studio and visiting morgues is method acting energy — Daniel Day-Lewis would be proud
- Turner painting storms he actually tied himself to a mast to witness = the original extreme content creator

### Episode 3 — Realism
- Courbet was the Kanye of 1850s Paris — massive ego, genuinely brilliant, couldn't stop telling everyone about it
- Building his own exhibition hall when the Salon rejected him = starting your own podcast after getting fired from a network
- Painting peasants at the scale of kings = making a prestige HBO series about fast food workers

### Episode 4 — Impressionism
- "Impressionism" started as an insult, like "punk" or "hip-hop" — the establishment named it to mock it, and the artists owned it
- If you've ever taken a photo on your phone and used a filter, you're dealing with the same question the Impressionists were: what does this moment actually look like vs. what does it feel like?
- The first independent exhibition = the art world equivalent of going direct-to-consumer, cutting out the middleman

### Episode 5 — Post-Impressionism
- Cézanne painting the same mountain 80 times = a musician doing 80 takes of the same song, each time hearing something different
- Van Gogh's letters to Theo = the most beautiful blog ever written, except nobody read it until he was dead
- Gauguin leaving his stockbroker job to paint in Tahiti = the 19th-century version of quitting tech to "find yourself" in Bali, except way more problematic

### Episode 6 — Symbolism + Art Nouveau
- Vienna 1900 had Freud, Klimt, and Mahler all in the same city = imagine if the most important psychologist, artist, and musician alive today all lived on the same block
- Art Nouveau trying to make everything beautiful — buildings, posters, furniture — is basically the Apple design philosophy a century early
- Loos writing "Ornament and Crime" = the original minimalist hot take, the Marie Kondo of architecture

## Running Notes
- Remember: silence on key paintings. Let them breathe.
- Always ground the art in what was happening politically/socially — these weren't people painting in a vacuum
- Keep coming back to the question: why should someone in 2025 care about this?
