# Music Cues — Episode 1: "The Man Who Wanted to Be Greek"

## General Rule
Silence during key artwork reveals and emotional peaks. Music for transitions and context-setting sections only.

## Cue Sheet

| Timecode (approx) | Section | Music | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0:00–2:30 | Cold open | None | Just voice. The vote scene needs to be raw. |
| 2:30–3:00 | Title/transition | Brief atmospheric swell | Transition into context section |
| 3:00–8:00 | Rococo & Winckelmann context | Light, period-appropriate underscore | Something elegant that can shift — Rococo lightness transitioning to something more austere |
| 8:00–8:30 | Transition to David | Brief swell or silence | |
| 8:30–18:00 | David's rise | Minimal underscore, building | Drop out entirely for Oath of the Horatii reveal |
| ~14:00 | Oath of the Horatii reveal | **SILENCE** | Hold 10–15 seconds. Let the painting speak. |
| 18:00–26:00 | Revolution section | Darker, tension-building underscore | Should feel like the stakes are rising |
| ~22:00 | Death of Marat reveal | **SILENCE** | 15–20 seconds minimum. This is the emotional peak. |
| 26:00–33:00 | Napoleon section | Something with grandeur but ironic edge | We're watching a man reinvent himself |
| 33:00–37:00 | Exile, death, legacy | Quiet, reflective | Winding down |
| 37:00–40:00 | Closing hook | Builds slightly — anticipation | Teasing Delacroix and Episode 2 |

## Music Style Notes
- Period-appropriate doesn't mean literal 18th-century music. It means *feeling*: strings, piano, restraint.
- Avoid anything bombastic during the Napoleon section — the irony should come from the contrast between calm music and grandiose imagery.
- The Revolution section could use something with a pulse — not drums, but rhythmic tension.
- Consider: Beethoven was alive during this entire period. His music literally soundtracks this era. Licensing classical recordings may be easier/cheaper than original composition.
