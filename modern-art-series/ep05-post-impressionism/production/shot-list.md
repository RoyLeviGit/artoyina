# Shot List — Episode 5: "The Lonely Ones Who Changed Everything"

## Full-Screen Artwork Holds

| Artwork | Duration | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Seurat — A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte | 8–10 sec | Bridge painting — let the scale read. Detail zoom: the dots up close. |
| Cézanne — Mont Sainte-Victoire (× 2–3 versions, side-by-side) | 6–8 sec each | Show the same mountain painted differently over 25 years — different angles, different planes |
| Cézanne — Still Life with Apples (tilting-table composition) | 6–8 sec | Emphasize the tilting perspective and shifted viewpoints |
| Cézanne — The Large Bathers | 8 sec | Hold on the triangular composition — bodies woven into landscape |
| Van Gogh — The Potato Eaters | 8 sec | The darkness. The hands. Faces rough and worn. |
| Van Gogh — The Starry Night | **10 sec silence** | Episode's first major emotional peak. Full silence — non-negotiable. |
| Gauguin — Vision After the Sermon (Jacob Wrestling the Angel) | 8 sec | The red ground. The flat stained-glass composition. No perspective. |
| Gauguin — Spirit of the Dead Watching | 8 sec | The figure, the watching spirit, the color. Note Teha'amana. |
| Gauguin — Where Do We Come From? What Are We? Where Are We Going? | 10 sec (slow pan) | Pan right to left (birth → death). Let the scale register. |
