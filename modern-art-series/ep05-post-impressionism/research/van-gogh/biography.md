# Vincent van Gogh (1853–1890)

## The Short Version
The most famous artist in history, and almost everything most people "know" about him is wrong or oversimplified. He wasn't a mad genius who painted in a frenzy. He was a deeply thoughtful, well-read, articulate man who struggled with mental illness and worked with extraordinary discipline. His letters to his brother Theo are among the greatest documents in art history. He sold very little in his lifetime. He made his entire mature body of work in about 10 years, the last 2.5 of which produced the paintings everyone knows.

## Life Timeline

### Before Painting (1853–1880)
- Born March 30, 1853, in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands. Son of a Protestant pastor.
- Worked as an art dealer (at Goupil & Cie, his uncle's firm) in The Hague, London, and Paris. Was fired.
- Tried to become a teacher. Failed.
- Became a lay preacher in the Borinage, a coal-mining region in Belgium. Lived among the miners in extreme poverty. The church dismissed him for being too zealous — he was giving away his clothes and food.
- At 27, decided to become an artist. He had about 10 years left to live.

### The Dutch Period (1880–1886)
- Self-taught, then briefly studied at academies in Brussels and Antwerp.
- Dark, earthy palette. Peasant subjects. Influenced by Millet (Episode 3 connection).
- **The Potato Eaters** (1885) — Five peasants eating potatoes by lamplight. Deliberately ugly. He wanted to show "that these people who are eating their potatoes by the light of their little lamp have dug the earth with the very hands they are putting into the dish."
- To Theo: "I want to do drawings which touch some people... I want to reach so far that people will say of my work: he feels deeply, he feels tenderly."

### Paris (1886–1888)
- Moved to Paris to live with Theo (who was now an art dealer).
- Everything changed. He saw Impressionist paintings. He saw Japanese prints. His palette exploded — from brown and grey to the vivid colors he's known for.
- Met Toulouse-Lautrec, Gauguin, Bernard, Signac, Pissarro.
- Painted over 200 works in two years. Self-portraits, cityscapes, still lifes.
- But Paris exhausted him. He drank too much. His health deteriorated.

### Arles (February 1888 – May 1889)
- Moved to Arles in southern France, seeking light and color.
- **The most productive period in art history.** In 15 months, he produced about 200 paintings and 100 drawings.
- *The Yellow House* — He rented it and dreamed of creating an artists' colony. Decorated it with paintings (the Sunflowers series was made to decorate Gauguin's room).
- *Bedroom in Arles* — His own room. Simple, colorful, slightly off-kilter. He wanted it to express "absolute restfulness."
- *Starry Night Over the Rhône*, *The Night Café*, *Café Terrace at Night* — all from this period.

### Gauguin Arrives (October–December 1888)
- Gauguin came to Arles at Theo's urging (Theo was Gauguin's dealer and hoped the arrangement would help both artists).
- Nine weeks together. They painted, argued, drank. Their artistic philosophies clashed: Van Gogh painted from observation; Gauguin painted from imagination and memory.
- **The ear incident (December 23, 1888):** After a violent argument, Van Gogh cut off part of his own left ear (the lower portion of the lobe, not the whole ear as myth has it). He wrapped it and delivered it to a woman at a local brothel. Gauguin left Arles the next day. They never saw each other again.
- Recent scholarship suggests the breakdown was triggered by news that Theo was getting engaged — Vincent feared losing his brother's emotional and financial support.

### Saint-Rémy (May 1889 – May 1890)
- Voluntarily admitted himself to the asylum at Saint-Paul-de-Mausole in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence.
- Continued painting between episodes of illness. Some of his greatest works come from here.
- **The Starry Night** (June 1889) — Painted from his asylum window (with the village added from imagination). Swirling sky, blazing stars, a dark cypress. It's the most reproduced painting in the world. He didn't think much of it — he preferred other works from this period.
- *Irises*, *Self-Portrait with Bandaged Ear*, *Wheatfield with Cypresses*

### Auvers-sur-Oise & Death (May–July 1890)
- Moved to Auvers, near Paris, under the care of Dr. Paul Gachet (an amateur painter and art collector).
- Painted 70 canvases in 70 days. Extraordinary productivity.
- **Wheatfield with Crows** — Often described as his last painting (it probably wasn't, but the myth persists). A turbulent sky, a path that leads nowhere, crows.
- On July 27, 1890, Van Gogh shot himself in the chest with a revolver (some recent scholarship questions whether it was self-inflicted, but the evidence is inconclusive). He walked back to the inn where he was staying. Theo rushed from Paris.
- Died July 29, 1890, with Theo at his side. He was 37.
- Theo, devastated, died six months later.

## The Letters
- Van Gogh wrote over 800 letters, mostly to Theo. They are extraordinary — articulate, passionate, detailed, funny, heartbreaking.
- They're not the ravings of a madman. They're the carefully considered thoughts of an intelligent, well-read man who happened to suffer from mental illness.
- The letters are the primary source. They should drive the narrative.

### Key Passages to Use

**On why he paints:**
> "I feel that there is nothing more truly artistic than to love people."

**On The Potato Eaters:**
> "I have tried to emphasize that those people, eating their potatoes in the lamplight, have dug the earth with those very hands they put in the dish, and so it speaks of manual labor, and how they have honestly earned their food."

**On color:**
> "Instead of trying to reproduce exactly what I see before me, I make more arbitrary use of color to express myself more forcefully."

**On Starry Night (to Theo):**
> "This morning I saw the countryside from my window a long time before sunrise, with nothing but the morning star, which looked very big."

**On his illness:**
> "If I could have worked without this accursed disease — what things I might have done."

**On death (to Theo, final letter, found in his pocket):**
> "Well, my own work, I am risking my life for it and my reason has half foundered because of it."

**On the future:**
> "What am I in the eyes of most people — a nonentity, an eccentric, or an unpleasant person... I should want my work to show what is in the heart of such an eccentric, such a nobody."

## Key Themes
1. **The man vs. the myth** — He wasn't a mad genius. He was a disciplined, thoughtful artist who suffered from mental illness. The letters prove this.
2. **The brother** — The Van Gogh story is really the story of two brothers. Without Theo's financial and emotional support, none of the art exists.
3. **Color as emotion** — Van Gogh used color not to describe what he saw but to express what he felt. "I make more arbitrary use of color to express myself more forcefully."
4. **Productivity and urgency** — He knew he didn't have much time. 70 paintings in 70 days at Auvers. The work pours out.
