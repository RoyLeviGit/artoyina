# Van Gogh's Letters — Selected Passages for the Script

These are organized thematically, not chronologically. Pick the ones that serve the story.

---

## On His Purpose
> "I feel that there is nothing more truly artistic than to love people." (Letter 248)

> "What am I in the eyes of most people — a nonentity, an eccentric, or an unpleasant person — somebody who has no position in society and will never have one; in short, the lowest of the low. All right, then — even if that were absolutely true, then I should one day like to show by my work what such an eccentric, such a nobody, has in his heart." (Letter 249)

> "I want to touch people with my art. I want them to say: he feels deeply, he feels tenderly." (Letter 218)

---

## On Color & Technique
> "Instead of trying to reproduce exactly what I see before me, I make more arbitrary use of color to express myself more forcefully." (Letter 663)

> "I am always in hope of making a discovery there, to express the love of two lovers by a wedding of two complementary colors, their mingling and their opposition, the mysterious vibrations of kindred tones." (Letter 531)

> "I want to paint men and women with that something of the eternal which the halo used to symbolize, and which we seek to convey by the actual radiance and vibration of our coloring." (Letter 531)

---

## On Specific Paintings
**The Potato Eaters:**
> "I have tried to emphasize that those people, eating their potatoes in the lamplight, have dug the earth with those very hands they put in the dish, and so it speaks of manual labor, and how they have honestly earned their food." (Letter 404)

**The Night Café:**
> "I have tried to express the terrible passions of humanity by means of red and green. The room is blood red and dark yellow with a green billiard table in the middle; there are four lemon-yellow lamps with a glow of orange and green. It is color not locally true from the point of view of the stereoscopic realist, but color suggesting some emotion of an ardent temperament." (Letter 676)

**Starry Night:**
> "This morning I saw the countryside from my window a long time before sunrise, with nothing but the morning star, which looked very big." (Letter 777)

**Bedroom in Arles:**
> "This time it's just simply my bedroom, only here color is to do everything, and giving by its simplification a grander style to things, is to be suggestive here of rest or of sleep in general." (Letter 705)

## On His Brother Theo
> "I should be desperate if my figures were correct... I do not want them to be academically correct... My great longing is to learn to make those very incorrectnesses, those deviations, remodellings, changes in reality, so that they may become, yes, lies if you like — but truer than the literal truth." (Letter 418)

> "Theo, I declare, I'd rather give up the whole thing than work without models." (Letter 251)

> "If I didn't have your friendship, I'd be driven to suicide without remorse." (Letter 587)

---

## On His Illness
> "If I could have worked without this accursed disease — what things I might have done." (Letter 863)

> "Oh, if I could have worked without this accursed disease, what things I might have done... isolated from others, following only the little light I feel in myself." (Letter 863)

> "The sadness will last forever." (Reported last words — attribution uncertain but widely cited)

---

## On Art & Life
> "I dream of painting and then I paint my dream." (Widely attributed, exact letter uncertain)

> "Well, my own work, I am risking my life for it and my reason has half foundered because of it — that's all right." (Letter 898 — found in his pocket after his death, unsent)

> "One may have a blazing hearth in one's soul and yet no one ever came to sit by it. Passers-by see only a wisp of smoke from the chimney and continue on their way." (Letter 155)
