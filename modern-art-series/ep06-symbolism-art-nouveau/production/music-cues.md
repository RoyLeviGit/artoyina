# Music Cues — Episode 6: "The Dreamers and the Decorators"

| Section | Music | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|

| After cold open / intro | Pulling back, mysterious | "We need to go back to the 1880s" — underscore settles |
| Act 2 — Symbolist turn | Interior, mysterious, slow | The inward turn. Not threatening — hypnotic. |
| Moreau — The Apparition | Uneasy, rich, strange | The jeweled darkness. Something beautiful and wrong. |
| Redon Noirs | **Near-silence / sparse** | The nightmares need room. Don't score them heavily. |
| Redon color transformation | Gradual brightening — emergence | The before/after. The music shifts as he shifts. |
| Bridge (Symbolism → Klimt) | Building, warming, golden | The temperature rises as we enter Vienna |
| Act 3 — Klimt early career | Conventional, then shifting | Mirrors his career arc — starts safe, becomes stranger |
| University paintings scandal | Tense, building | The confrontation. "Enough of censorship." |
| The Vienna Secession | Defiant, proud | The break. The motto. The building. |
| Beethoven Frieze — pan | Grand, choral, Beethoven-adjacent | "This kiss to the whole world" — let it swell |
| Golden phase intro | Rich, warm, golden | Shifting into the gold section |
| Adele Bloch-Bauer I | Warm, sacred, shimmering | Sacred and sexual at the same time — music should feel that tension |
| The Kiss | **Full silence — 10 seconds** | Non-negotiable. Then something sparse and transcendent. |
| Emilie / Klimt's death | Intimate, quiet | "Send for Emilie." One of the series' quietest moments. |
| Bridge (Klimt → Art Nouveau) | Lightening, more playful | The tone shift — from gallery to street |
| Act 4 — Art Nouveau | Light, decorative, energy | The democratic energy of the movement |
| Mucha posters | Light, graphic, quick | The overnight celebrity — something almost commercial |
| Gaudí — Sagrada Família | Large, organic, sacred | Architecture as nature as religion |
| Casa Batlló / Park Güell | More playful, color-like | The fantasy buildings. Joy. |
| Horta — Hôtel Tassel | Elegant, curving, Art Nouveau-y | The whiplash curve in sound |
| Act 5 — The Backlash | Stripped down, clean | Loos wins the sound design. Minimal. |
| "Ornament and Crime" quote | **Brief silence or near-silence** | Let the title land |
| Act 6 — The big recap | Building from quiet to enormous | Six movements recapped — each one should feel different, brief |
| The list (Picasso, Matisse, Duchamp, Kandinsky) | Surging, electric, forward | The future rushing toward you |
| Final beat — "But that's another story" | Dropping away to near-silence | End on something small and human |
| End credits | Something that recaps the emotional arc of the series | Last thing the viewer hears |

## Silence Moments (Non-Negotiable)
1. The Kiss — 10 seconds full silence
2. Redon Noirs — near-silence
3. "Ornament and Crime" title — brief pause to let it land
