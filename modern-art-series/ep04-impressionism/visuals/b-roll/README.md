## Notes
- **Morisot portrait** is low-res (803px). Use as a brief 2–3 second insert only. No higher-res public-domain version available.
- **Bazille portrait** is a self-portrait painting, not a photograph — appropriate given Bazille died in 1870 (Franco-Prussian War) before photography was widespread for artists.
- **Nadar self-portrait** doubles as a reference to the photographer who hosted the first Impressionist exhibition in 1874 at his Boulevard des Capucines studio.
- **Marville photo:** Contextualizes the Haussmann transformation of Paris that allowed the Impressionists to paint the new urban life.
- **Paint tubes:** Modern paint tubes with metal collapsible design — introduced 1841, enabling plein air painting.
